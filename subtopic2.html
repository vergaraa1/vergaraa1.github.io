Artificial Intelligence in Severe Weather and Wildfire Modeling
Site Author
 Brian Bothwell


Abstract
 Bothwell from the Government Accountability Office discusses how predictive technology, machine learning, can affect policy-making. For example, the National Weather Service can use this to be better informed on natural disasters and how to prepare citizens to take the appropriate precautionary measures. This can reduce the uncertainty currently seen when tracking storms, potentially saving lives and money in damages. Implementing machine learning into weather forecasting results in improved data in comparison to traditional data collection models, picking up on relationships between recorded data not previously recognized. In government agencies, machine learning can assist in organization, allowing for these sectors to focus on their mission.

 Artificial Intelligence: The Ambiguous Labor Market Impact of Automating Prediction
Site Author
 Ajay Agrawal, Joshua S. Gans, Avi Goldfarb
Abstract
 Continuing on the topic of machine learning, this journal explores how this can be implemented in other fields. For example, small law firms are testing out predictive language and how this can affect verdicts in the courts. This does not impact all aspects of human cognition, but solely focuses on prediction. Without a decision to be made, prediction is useless. Those with prediction-based careers, such as translating and human resources, may find their role in their careers to be diminished with this advancement, while others, such as the legal field or policy makers, may find this to enhance their jobs.

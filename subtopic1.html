 How the United States Competed: Past Technology Strategies
Site Author
 Martijn Rasser and Megan Lamberth
Abstract
 This journal publication discusses past policies that have pushed technological advancements in the United States. Policies that advance this movement further are outlined by five points: whole-of-nation, flexible, iterative, and multilateral. For example, the Bayh-Dole Act and the Federal Technology Transfer Act of 1980 allocate federal funding to universities and research organizations to use federal laboratories, opening new forms of innovations not possible at their current facilities. In addition, they own the rights and patents to their works regardless of their dependence on federal funding and facilities. When the country invests in its innovation and education, its citizens are motivated to create concepts that can drastically change daily activities.
